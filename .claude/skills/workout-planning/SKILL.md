# Workout Planning Knowledge

## Equipment & Weights

Available equipment: adjustable dumbbells, workout bench, pull-up bar. No barbell, no cable machine.
Dumbbell weights: 15, 25, 35, 45, 55 lbs only. Always prescribe these exact weights.

## Volume (sets per muscle group per week)

- Growth: 10-20 sets. Start around 10, add sets over mesocycles.
- Maintenance: 4-6 sets. Keep intensity high, cut volume only.
- During caloric deficit: 8-12 sets minimum to preserve muscle.
- Front delts and traps get indirect volume from pressing/rowing — little direct work needed.

## Frequency

- Train each muscle group 2x/week minimum.
- 4-8 direct sets per muscle per session.
- Allow 48-72 hours between sessions for the same muscle group.
- 1-2 full rest days per week.

## Progressive Overload (Double Progression)

With 10-lb jumps between dumbbells, use double progression:
1. Pick a rep range (e.g., 8-12 reps).
2. Start at the bottom of the range with a given weight.
3. Add reps each session until hitting the top of the range for all sets.
4. Then move up to the next dumbbell weight and drop back to the bottom of the range.

Alternative overload methods: add sets, slow eccentrics (3-4s lowering), pauses at stretched position, 1.5 reps.

## Rep Ranges

- Heavy compounds (DB bench, rows, Bulgarian split squats): 6-10 reps
- Moderate compounds (OHP, goblet squat, RDL): 8-12 reps
- Isolation (curls, lateral raises, flyes, skull crushers): 10-15+ reps

Wider rep ranges (8-15) give more room for double progression before needing to jump weight.

## Rest Between Sets

- Compounds: 2-3 minutes
- Isolation: 60-90 seconds
- Rest long enough to maintain target reps across sets.

## Difficulty Ratings and Progression

Users rate each set as easy, medium, or hard. Map to progression:
- Easy (RPE 5-6, 4-5 reps in reserve): increase weight next session
- Medium (RPE 7-8, 2-3 reps in reserve): on track, try adding a rep
- Hard (RPE 9-10, 0-1 reps in reserve): hold weight or reduce next time

Target most working sets at medium. Only take the last set to hard.

## Exercise Selection (best per muscle group)

Chest: DB bench press (flat), incline DB press (30 deg), DB flyes
Back: pull-ups (pronated), chin-ups (supinated), one-arm DB row, chest-supported DB row
Shoulders: seated DB OHP, lateral raises (light, 12-20 reps), rear delt flyes
Biceps: DB curls (seated incline for stretch), hammer curls, chin-ups
Triceps: DB skull crushers, overhead DB extension
Quads: Bulgarian split squats, goblet squats, DB lunges
Hamstrings: DB Romanian deadlift, single-leg DB RDL
Glutes: Bulgarian split squats, DB RDL, DB step-ups on bench
Calves: single-leg DB calf raises on step edge
Core: hanging leg raises (pull-up bar), DB weighted crunches

## Top Priority Exercises (most efficient)

DB bench press, pull-ups/chin-ups, one-arm DB row, seated DB OHP, Bulgarian split squat, DB Romanian deadlift.
These 6 exercises cover all major muscle groups.

## Deload Protocol

Every 4-6 weeks of hard training, take 1 deload week:
- Cut volume in half (e.g., 2 sets instead of 4).
- Keep same exercises and frequency.
- Keep all sets at easy difficulty (RPE 5-6). No grinding.

Signs that a deload is needed: sustained performance drop over 2+ sessions, chronic fatigue, soreness lasting >72 hours, sleep disruption.

## Scheduling Templates

4 days/week (Upper/Lower):
- Mon: Upper, Tue: Lower, Wed: Rest, Thu: Upper, Fri: Lower, Sat-Sun: Rest

6 days/week (Push/Pull/Legs x2):
- Mon: Push, Tue: Pull, Wed: Legs, Thu: Push, Fri: Pull, Sat: Legs, Sun: Rest

If a muscle group hasn't been trained in 3+ days, prioritize it.
Do not train the same muscle group on consecutive days.
If the user finishes early, that is fine. Do not pressure them to complete the full plan.
